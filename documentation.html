Portfolio Notes

  • I am intending for this portfolio to grab the attention of employers, enabling them to see both who I am and what I have been learning during my time at RIT.
  • My idea was for this portfolio to be aligned with the requirements for this project while still maintaining a unique experience for site visitors.
  • I would like to include a CSS flexbox and grid to organize and optimize my content, a scrolling navigation to move around the page, links to relevant contact information, and images meant to showcase my prior or upcoming work.
  • My content will be organized with a one-page layout in mind. Every component on my page will be flexible to accommodate mobile devices. My external stylesheet will accomplish all of my style choices and possible corrections. Both my HTML and CSS will need to pass validation as well.

Design Notes

  • I attempted to make good use of borders and horizontal rules to make both section divisions and images pop-out for the viewer of my portfolio.
  • One of the critiques that I received was to make a “dark mode” for my website, featuring darker backgrounds and lighter text. After I incorporated this design, I realized how much more appealing my website will be for night time viewers.
  • Another suggestion was for me to make my navigation button scroll to their respective sections, which helps promote a smooth flow of content as opposed to more “jumpy” page links.
  • I made sure to make my margins and padding consistent to help in my repetition of similar content sections. Viewers will hopefully appreciate that my portfolio possesses consistent spacing and makes efficient use of a single-page layout.
  • Although the text for the project section is currently filler, I took care in making sure the formatting matches the rest of the neighboring content. I did this by making the entire section a CSS grid, but the interior subsections a flexbox container. This gives me the benefits of an adjustable container, while interior pieces stay connected to one another regardless of size.
  • My resume is a PDF embedded within the page. I used the embed tag in combination with an image tag to accomplish this.
  • I desired to be consistent in my use of colors, so I adhered to a strict scheme throughout the web page. This does not include images used to identify my project, but possibly could in the future.
  • Image dimensions were definitely considered for making my page. Every image is under 500kb, and is resized through CSS to fit its container.
